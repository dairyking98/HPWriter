### 3D Printing Specifications

**Material Recommendations:**
- **Primary Material**: PETG (recommended)
  - Good strength and durability
  - Reasonable print temperatures
  - Good layer adhesion
  - Suitable for functional prototypes
- **Alternative Material**: ABS
  - Higher temperature resistance
  - Good mechanical properties
  - Requires heated bed and enclosure
- **Not Recommended for Prototype**: PLA (too brittle, low temp resistance)

**Print Settings (Typical):**
- **Layer Height**: 0.2-0.3 mm (0.2 mm for fine details)
- **Infill**: 20-30% (structural parts may need 40-50%)
- **Walls/Perimeters**: 3-4 layers
- **Support**: Required for overhangs >45Â°
- **Build Plate**: Heated bed recommended (60-80Â°C for PETG)
- **Nozzle Temperature**: 230-250Â°C (PETG), 240-260Â°C (ABS)
- **Bed Adhesion**: Brim or raft recommended for large flat parts

**Post-Processing:**
- Remove support material
- Sand smooth surfaces (especially for moving parts)
- Test fit with hardware (screws, bearings, etc.)
- Optional: Vapor smoothing (ABS) or surface finishing

**File Formats:**
- CAD Source: STEP files (from Fusion 360, SolidWorks, etc.)
- 3D Printer: STL files (exported from CAD)
- Slicer: G-code (generated by slicer software)
